Test Name Purpose Normal Range Abnormal Results
Complete Blood Count (CBC) Determines the number and type of blood cells present in the sample Varies based on age and gender, but typically:
- Red Blood Cells (RBC): 4.5-5.5 million cells/mcL
- White Blood Cells (WBC): 4,500-11,000 cells/mcL
- Hemoglobin (Hb): 13.5-17.5 g/dL for males; 12-15.5 g/dL for females
- Hematocrit (Hct): 38.8%-50% for males; 34.9%-44.5% for females
- Platelets: 150,000-450,000/mcL 		- Low RBC count: may indicate anemia or bleeding
- High WBC count: may indicate infection or inflammation
- Low Hb or Hct: may indicate anemia
- High Hb or Hct: may indicate dehydration or polycythemia
- Low platelet count: may indicate bleeding or a blood disorder
Test Name Purpose Normal Range Abnormal Results
Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP) Measures several electrolytes and other substances in the blood to assess kidney function and other metabolic processes - Sodium (Na): 135-145 mEq/L
- Potassium (K): 3.5-5.1 mEq/L
- Chloride (Cl): 98-106 mEq/L
- Bicarbonate (HCO3): 22-28 mEq/L
- Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN): 7-20 mg/dL
- Creatinine (Cr): 0.6-1.3 mg/dL 		- Low or high levels of any electrolyte: may indicate a metabolic disorder or kidney dysfunction
- High BUN or creatinine levels: may indicate kidney dysfunction
